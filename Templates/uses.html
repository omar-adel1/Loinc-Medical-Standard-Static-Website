Lab and clinical (uses)

Both ordering and interpreting test results can benefit from LOINC codes. For conveying laboratory and clinical findings in HL7 messages, LOINC is widely recognized and recommended. LOINC is widely used in laboratory and clinical uses. The following is a small (non-exhaustive) list of suggestions:

  • HL7 Standards Development Organization designated LOINC as a recommended code set for laboratory test names in transactions between health care institutions, laboratories, laboratory testing devices, and public health agencies in 1999.

  • LOINC was accepted by the Consolidated Health Informatics project to standardize the electronic transmission of clinical laboratory data and federally mandated patient assessment questionnaires with material on functionality and disability.

  • The HIPAA Administrative Simplification: Standards for Electronic Health Care Claims proposed regulation When extra paperwork (also known as health care claim attachments) is necessary, Attachments suggests standards that will ease the electronic interchange of clinical and administrative data to improve the claims adjudication process. The regulation suggests using the LOINC to identify the extra information that is being sought and the coded responses that are provided in response to the requests.
