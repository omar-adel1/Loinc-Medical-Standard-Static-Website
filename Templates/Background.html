Background

The Logical Observation Identifiers, Names, and Codes (LOINC) standard supports the interchange and pooling of clinical care, outcomes management, and research findings, such as laboratory tests or vital signs. LOINC is a global coding system that allows healthcare providers to map (or connect) their local codes to LOINC codes for transmission in clinical communications to payers, regulators, and other providers. Because LOINC codes are based on a six-part name, they are unique not only to the component (e.g., hemoglobin) but also to the specimen (e.g., whole blood) and other elements that constitute a separate result. The Regenstrief Institute, with sponsorship from the US National Library of Medicine, maintains and distributes the LOINC standard for free (NLM). The American Clinical Laboratory Association and the College of American Pathologists have both supported LOINC. It's also part of the National Library of Medicine's Unified Medical Language System (UMLS), and it's been adopted by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), the Department of Defense (DoD), and the Department of Veterans Affairs. The Regenstrief LOINC Mapping Assistant (RELMA) is a Windows-based tool that helps businesses translate their local institution codes to the LOINC standard. The software may be downloaded for free from the LOINC website. Workshops on LOINC and how to utilize the RELMA tool are frequently presented at the Regenstrief Institute's semiannual public meetings. To find out when and where the next meeting will take place, go to the LOINC website.
